The Baseline

What's Working Well

The foundation is strong. The creative and brand positioning are already in a great place.


  • Premium Aesthetic: Dark backgrounds, clean product photography, and consistent clinical branding look excellent.
  • Multi-Flow Architecture: Welcome, Browse Abandonment, and Consultation flows are all actively running.
  • Smart Content Strategy: Each flow has a distinct job (e.g., brand education vs. product retargeting).
  • High-Converting Social Proof: The "Real people, real results" email is phenomenal using age, specific treatments, and clear timelines builds immense trust.
Gap 01

Flow Collisions & Suppression

Critical Issue

Between 4:26 PM and 4:34 PM on April 29, three separate emails were delivered within an 8-minute window.

  • 4:26 PM: Mother's Day Campaign
  • 4:31 PM: Browse Abandonment Flow
  • 4:34 PM: Consultation Flow

The Impact: Without a careful send strategy, perhaps including smart sending, campaigns and flows fire blindly. A customer receiving 3 emails in 8 minutes is highly likely to unsubscribe or report as spam.

It would be beneficial to pull a frequency report to ensure people aren't getting too many emails within a short amount of time. I dive deeper into this topic in my recent LinkedIn post ↗.

Gap 02

Broken Preview Text

Critical Issue

Almost every flow email is leaking raw HTML code into the inbox preview text. Note: I only noticed this on the desktop version; mobile appeared fine.

XYON" title="XYON" height="auto"> XYON. Start your consultation.
  • The desktop preheader is pulling from a broken alt tag on a header image rather than intentionally set copy.
  • The Fix: Audit the templates in Klaviyo and hardcode a hidden, intentional preheader text block.
Gap 03

Rigid Welcome Cadence

Optimization Opportunity

The Welcome Flow operates on a strict 24-hour timer, ignoring user behavior in between sends.


  • Email 1 triggers immediately.
  • Email 2 triggers exactly 24 hours later.

Strategic Shift: It may be worth testing a behavioral branch here. If a user starts a consultation between Email 1 and Email 2, they could be filtered out of the generic Welcome Flow and moved exclusively into the Consultation Flow.

Gap 04

Aggressive Browse Abandonment

Optimization Opportunity

Two Browse Abandonment emails were sent for the exact same product (DHT-Blocking Shampoo), spaced only 2 hours apart.


  • While cross-selling the broader routine in the second touch is a great tactic, a 2-hour delay is likely too aggressive.
  • The Fix: Space the second touch to 24-48 hours, and add logic to reset the timer if the user returns to the site between emails.
Gap 05

Inconsistent Templates

Brand Experience

Different flows are using entirely different footer layouts and sender addresses.


  • Welcome & Consultation Flows: List C/O Blakes, Cassels & Graydon LLP and feature 6 social icons.
  • Browse Abandonment Flow: Lists 18 W 4th Ave, Vancouver and features 4 social icons.

This indicates the flows were likely built at different times and never standardized, creating a slightly fractured brand experience.

How can I potentially help?

Retention Focus

The Month 2-4 Onboarding Gap

Strategy Overview

Hair loss treatments take 3-6 months to show visible results. Assuming this doesn't exist already, the highest churn risk exists between Month 2 and Month 4 when patients are paying but haven't seen changes yet.


  • Month 1: "What's happening beneath the surface."
  • Month 2: "Shedding is a normal part of the process."
  • Month 3: "What to start looking for."

The Takeaway: Education is the strongest churn prevention. If patients understand the medical timeline upfront, they stay subscribed long enough to see the results.

Copy Improvements

Injecting Emotional Resonance

Strategy Overview

To maximize retention, the copy needs to deeply resonate with the patient's internal monologue and unspoken pain points.


  • Acknowledge the Pain: The frustration of always feeling like you have to wear a hat, struggling to look people in the face, or constantly comparing yourself to the "perfect" guys on TikTok.
  • Sell the Transformation: Shift the core focus from just regaining hair to regaining confidence and control.

The Goal: Use copy that speaks directly to what they are already thinking in their head, building a stronger, more emotional bond with the brand.

Execution Roadmap

The First 90 Days

Strategy Overview

Based on what I know now. This may change based on the information the team tells me.


  • Days 1-30: Complete a full Klaviyo audit, fix immediate flow collisions, and build the core Onboarding sequence.
  • Days 31-60: Launch dedicated Churn Prevention and Win-Back flows. Pull a frequency report to ensure patients aren't receiving unnecessary frequent emails.
  • Days 61-90: Review existing cohort tracking data, begin A/B testing timing/messaging, and actively optimize for long-term LTV.
